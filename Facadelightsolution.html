Facade Lighting

Linear Light Facade

Linear light facades use slim LED strips to accentuate architectural elements. They enhance a building's visibility and aesthetic appeal, especially at night. Common placements include edges, corners, joints, and around windows. These lights can be recessed, surface-mounted, or integrated into cladding. RGB and RGBW LEDs offer dynamic color-changing effects. They're energy-efficient and suitable for long-term outdoor use. Weather-resistant (IP-rated) fixtures ensure durability in all climates. Lighting controls allow for programmable effects and automation. Ideal for commercial, residential, and landmark buildings. Linear lighting transforms a facade into a vibrant nighttime feature.

Dot Light Facade

Dot light facades use individual point light sources (LED nodes) arranged across a building surface. Each "dot" acts as a pixel, enabling low-resolution video or graphic displays. They are often used for dynamic media facades and interactive lighting effects. Dot lights can be arranged in grids, curves, or custom patterns. RGB or RGBW LEDs allow full-color illumination and animations. Each dot is typically addressable for precise control over brightness and color. They are lightweight, flexible, and can adapt to complex architectural surfaces. Weatherproof designs (e.g., IP67) ensure performance in harsh environments. Control systems like DMX or SPI enable synchronized content playback. Dot light facades create eye-catching visuals for stadiums, malls, and skyscrapers.
